There are 5\% defective items in a large bulk of items. What is the probability that a sample of 10 items will include not more than one defective item?

\solution

Let 
\begin{align}
	 X =
	\begin{cases}
		1, & \text{sample contains 1 defective item}\\ 
		0, & \text{sample contains no defective item}
	\end{cases}
\end{align}
Then  
\begin{align}
               p_X\brak{1} &= \frac{5}{100}\\
               &= 0.05\\
               p_X\brak{0} &= 1-p_X\brak{1}\\
               &=1-\frac{5}{100}\\
               &=\frac{95}{100}\\
               &= 0.95
\end{align}

We use binomial distribution to solve this question, whose formula is given by
\[
\pr{X=k} = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1 - p)^{n - k}
\]
where $n$ is the number of trials, $k$ is the number of successful outcomes, and $p$ is the probability of single successful outcome

As defective rate is 5\%,
$p = 0.05$
We need to find the probability of having 0 or 1 defective items in a sample of 10.

For k = 0;
\begin{align}
        \pr{X=0}&= \binom{10}{0}* 0.05^0 *(1 - 0.05)^{10 - 0}\\
        &= 1*1*0.95^{10}\\
        &= 0.5987
\end{align}

For k = 1;
\begin{align}
        \pr{X=1}&= \binom{10}{1}* 0.05^1 *(1 - 0.05)^{10 - 1}\\
        &= 10*0.05*0.95^9\\
        &= 0.3151
\end{align}

To find the probability of event E which is not having more than 1 defective item, we sum the probabilities of having 0 or 1 defective items.
\begin{align}
        \pr{E}&= \pr{X=0} + \pr{X=1}\\
        &= 0.5987 + 0.3151\\
        &= 0.9138
\end{align}
